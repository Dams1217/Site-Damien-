About

I am Damien Vonlanthen. Author, screenwriter, actor.

I started writing because some things cannot be expressed any other way. There were heavy silences, fragile memories, truths that were too uncomfortable. Writing became a necessity — a vital act, a way of holding reality in tension.

My journey is unique. I have no formal artistic background. My path is forged by lived experience, emotional depth, and a burning need to express what words often fail to capture. This is my first film — but many others are already on their way. I am also collaborating with various directors on projects as an actor. Nothing has yet been filmed, but everything is already alive.

Destiny led me here. Every project I undertake is a part of a much larger process: a personal evolution, a transmission, a connection. I write from what I carry. And I act from what I’ve survived.

Artistic Approach

I work on narratives where intimate wounds intersect with collective memory. My films explore silence, emotional legacy, and hidden grief. I write as a way to heal, to share, and to leave a trace of what might otherwise disappear.

I also write road trip and action stories — narratives with movement, intensity, sometimes with no comforting resolution. But whether still or explosive, everything I create comes from the same place: a deeply rooted emotional truth.

Universe

I’m drawn to fragile characters, survivors, silent heirs of past generations. My cinematic universe speaks in nuances, in shadow and stillness. It’s not there to explain — it’s there to make you feel.

I believe cinema can be powerful without being loud. I search for meaning behind glances, for stories behind absences. My films question, disturb, and — I hope — leave something behind once the screen goes dark.

Influences

I admire filmmakers like Xavier Dolan, Asghar Farhadi, Nanni Moretti, the Dardenne brothers. Artists who capture the invisible with rare humanity. Their work helped me understand that emotion is a language in itself — and one that I want to master.

In writing, I’m influenced by the rawness of Annie Ernaux, the introspection of Marguerite Duras, and the cinematic energy of American screenwriting. I stand between the poetic and the visceral — because both are needed to tell the truth.

Works

HERITIERS (in development): A feature film about emotional legacy and unspeakable truths passed down through generations. A story of silence, family, and inner revolt. This is the beginning — but not the end. Other scripts are in the works, and collaborations as an actor are multiplying. It’s only the beginning of a much deeper journey.
